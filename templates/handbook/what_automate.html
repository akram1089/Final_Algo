Automated Trading in Finance

It is important to keep up with the latest trends and technologies in finance to stay competitive. For stock and options traders, this means keeping up with technological advances in trading.

Automated Trading Defined

Automated trading, also known as algorithmic trading or algo trading, uses software (also known as bots or robots) to automatically place trades based on preset criteria.

Autotrading strategies use a series of decisions to assess real-time market data and make trading decisions based on defined rules. Trades automatically execute when the conditions are met.

Strategies are often backtested against historical data to ensure proof of concept.

You can manually enter your trade orders into your computer or smartphone. Or, you can use automated trading software and let computers do all of the work for you.

Hybrid strategies combine manual and automated trading where either entries or exits are executed manually by the trader and automation executes other parts of the trading plan.

Automated trading platforms provide a framework for scaling a portfolio with speed and efficiency. The platforms have evolved rapidly in recent years and offer a wide range of features and capabilities.

Who can use automated trading?

All types of traders can use and benefit from autotrading. Some platforms are designed for retail investors, while others are geared towards institutional traders.

Why use automated trading?

The two main benefits of autotrading are the ability to remove emotion from your trading decisions and scale your portfolio.

When you automate your trades, the mundane, repetitive trading tasks are offloaded, removing opportunities for human error. Automation minimizes the tasks where human input is error-prone.

Autotrading allows you to scale your portfolio by allocating your capital to multiple trades or strategies at once. This is especially useful for trading a large number of securities or a portfolio that uses strategies across multiple timeframes.

Manually trading multiple strategies divides attention and increases the potential for missed opportunities. Automated trading, on the other hand, can scan for opportunities and monitor positions faster and easier than any human.
